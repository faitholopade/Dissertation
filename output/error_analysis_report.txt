  2. Under-classification / Missed domains (43 instances)
     The automated method labelled 'unknown' when the manual
     annotator identified a clear Annex III domain.  Common causes:
     sparse incident descriptions and implicit rather than explicit
     domain language (e.g. 'public assistance' not 'welfare').

  3. Domain boundary confusion (16 instances)
     Employment ↔ essential services swaps.  Reflects genuine
     ambiguity at the Annex III boundary — e.g. a government HR
     tool could fall under Annex III/4 or Annex III/5a.

  4. Partial match — dual-domain records (24 instances)
     Manual annotator marked BOTH employment AND essential services
     but automated method only predicted one.  Suggests the LLM
     defaults to a single-label framing.

  5. Rights label disagreements (78 instances)
     Most common pattern: LLM predicts 'OTHER' where manual found
     specific rights (NON-DISCRIMINATION, PRIVACY), or LLM adds
     extra rights categories the manual annotator did not flag.
